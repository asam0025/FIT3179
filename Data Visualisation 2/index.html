Esports Competitions: The Rise of Competitive Gaming

Over the past decade, esports has transformed from niche tournaments into one of the most profitable and widely viewed forms of competition. Behind every match lies numerous players, teams, and games competing for millions in prizes and an ever-growing fanbase. This visualisation takes you through how esports has achieved it's global reach and the teams and titles that define the scene.

A Global Stage for Competition

Esports is no longer limited to a few gaming hubs. It has grown to become a worldwide arena. The map below reveals how prize money flows across regions, showing where competition is fiercest. Countries like China, the United States, and Western Europe dominate the scene, reflecting the infrastructure and investment in professional gaming.

The Heart of Esports Growth

Behind every competition is an audience that makes it matter. The treemap below illustrates how viewership has evolved across major titles and years. As the chart shows, audience engagement follows the same pattern as prize money, where they are concentrated in a few leading titles, but with new games emerging to challenge the giants.

As these visualisations demonstrate, esports has become a monolith in gaming culture where global reach, diverse gaming titles, strategic team building, and passionate viewership merge to create one of the fastest growing entertainment industries of our time.
